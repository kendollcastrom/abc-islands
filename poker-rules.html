Poker Game

What happens if I disconnect in the middle of one hand?
Occasionally it happens that due to reasons that are beyond the control of our players, they cannot act in time on their poker hand. A player in a cash game (non-tournament) typically has 25 seconds to act with his hand if it is a fixed limit poker game and 35 seconds in a pot-limit or no limit poker game. A warning message is displayed in the chat box with 15 seconds left to act. If there was a disconnection and a player returned before timeout, he or she is given at least 10 seconds to act. Less time is allocated to perform at designated "fast" tables.
If a player is unable to perform within the allotted time while connected, the system bends the hand.
Additional time may be allocated to perform in some games, depending on the situation. The amount of extra time may vary depending on the game, but the concept is the same at all times: when faced with a decision on a larger pot, more time is given to return due to a disconnection.

Please note that tournaments do not allocate additional time due to disconnections, in most situations.

What happens if the system crashes in the middle of a game?
All transactions, including jackpots won and lost, are recorded in your account at the end of each hand. In the unlikely event of a server crash preventing the completion of a hand, the hands in progress at each table (tournaments and cash games) will be restored by pushing back these hands as if they had not happened. Each player's chip count will be reset to the amount at the beginning of the hand.

The poker game I wish to join is full. What should I do?
If a poker game is full, you can put your name on the waiting list for that game. When an in-game seat becomes available, the first person on the waiting list will have the first right of rejection for that seat.

The other option you have is to sit at a table empty of that limit and game. Often, empty tables fill up quickly once a player takes a seat.

What is a "rake"? How much does rake cost in poker games?
In our poker games, you never play against the house, only against other players. We receive compensation by taking a rake, a small amount from each cash pot. This jackpot rake never exceeds $5. At the lower limits, we take a smaller rake.

There is no rake "by hand" in tournaments; There is only an initial fee to enter.

How do I know how much money is in the pot?
At any of our tables, the total jackpot amount, including all current bets, are always displayed numerically on the table. Also, at the end of each betting round, all bets are collected in the center of the table and converted into higher denomination chips so you can easily see the total jackpot value.

What is the difference between No Limit, Pot Limit and Fixed Limit poker?
The betting structure for a particular game sets the limits of how much you can bet each time you have to act and in the case of CAP games, how much you can bet in total on each hand.
In poker games with an unlimited betting structure, each player can bet or increase by any amount up to and including their full stack (the total number of chips they possess at any given time) in any betting round, provided it is their turn to Act. In poker games with a jackpot limit betting structure, each player can bet or increase by any amount up to the size of the total jackpot at that time. In poker games with a fixed limit betting structure, each player can choose to match, bet or increase, but only for a fixed amount.
